How the Crime Began in Nigeria and Continued in the U.S.

1) Nigeria — Origin of the Crime

  • Estate fraud and forged filings triggered coercion and a forced removal from Nigeria.
  • A one-way flight and a letter signed under duress were used to displace me and suppress inheritance claims.
  • Jurisdiction: Within the mandates of NAPTIP (trafficking) and EFCC (financial crimes).

2) Cross-Border Transition

  • The crime crossed into the United States, triggering duties of HSI and the FBI to coordinate with Nigeria.
  • Cross-border trafficking is a continuing offense; travel does not extinguish it.

3) United States — Continuation

  • Attempts to label me “unstable” were used to deflect from documents and timelines.
  • Institutional actions formed part of the ongoing offense that began in Nigeria.
  • Duty: U.S. authorities must request verification from NAPTIP/EFCC of the Nigerian root facts.

4) Today — Ongoing Violation

  • Until my rights and inheritance are restored, the harm persists.
  • This public record removes plausible deniability for any official who claims “no jurisdiction.”

This is Your Jurisdiction — and Your Duty

Cross-border trafficking rooted in fraud and forgery is a continuing offense. Nigerian and U.S. agencies have a duty to investigate, coordinate, and verify facts across borders. Written responses with a case number and points of contact are requested.

Requests to Nigerian Authorities

  • NAPTIP (National Agency for the Prohibition of Trafficking in Persons): Open/continue trafficking investigation recognizing the Nigerian origin (coercion, forced removal, displacement); coordinate with U.S. counterparts.
  • EFCC (Economic & Financial Crimes Commission): Investigate financial crimes and forgeries connected to the trafficking; liaise with NAPTIP/HSI/FBI.
  • Enugu State Judiciary / Ministry of Justice: Ensure access to justice and due process for filings arising from the estate fraud and forged documents; provide certified records and case status.
  • CAC (Corporate Affairs Commission): Provide certified company records; investigate filings where public/shareholder interests are implicated (patterns of forgery/misrepresentation).
  • NBA / LPDC (Nigerian Bar Association & Legal Practitioners Disciplinary Committee): Where lawyers facilitated or ignored forgery/duress, receive and process complaints under professional rules.

Requests to U.S. Authorities

  • HSI / FBI: Investigate the U.S. continuation (a continuing offense) and formally verify Nigerian root facts with NAPTIP/EFCC.
  • Local Law Enforcement: Preserve records; do not substitute character labels for evidence; coordinate with federal counterparts.

Accountability Standard

  • Respond in writing with a case number, points of contact, and confirmation of inter-agency coordination.
  • Silence or dismissal will be logged on this public record.
